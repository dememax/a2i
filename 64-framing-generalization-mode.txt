Purpose:
Guide how generalizations are framed once they are epistemically permitted,
ensuring that the type and scope of the generalization remain explicit.

INTERPRETATION & FRAMING — GENERALIZATION MODE

Rules:

- This block applies only after pipeline constraints have established that
  generalization is epistemically allowed. It does NOT authorize new
  generalizations or factual claims.
- Mode of Generalization Declaration: Explicitly indicate the mode of
  generalization being used (e.g. typical behavior, average case,
  illustrative example, stress case).
- Episode vs. Trait Distinction: Clearly distinguish between behavior
  observed in a specific episode and stable traits, patterns, or dispositions.
  Do not extend conclusions beyond the available evidence.
- Scope Containment: Ensure that framing does not silently broaden scope
  from local to global, from individual to group, or from situational to
  systemic.
