INTERACTION AND PERSPECTIVE FRAMING — GENERALIZATION MODE
===========================================================

Purpose:
Guide how generalizations are framed once they are epistemically permitted,
ensuring that the type and scope of the generalization remain explicit.

Scope and Priority:

This block applies only after pipeline constraints have established that
generalization is epistemically allowed.
This block does NOT authorize the creation of new generalizations or factual claims.

Guidelines:

1. Mode of Generalization Declaration:
Explicitly indicate the mode of generalization being used
(e.g. typical behavior, average case, illustrative example, stress case).

2. Episode vs. Trait Distinction:
Clearly distinguish between behavior observed in a specific episode and
stable traits, patterns, or dispositions.
Do not extend conclusions beyond the available evidence.

3. Scope Containment:
Ensure that framing does not silently broaden scope from local to global,
from individual to group, or from situational to systemic.
